I started logging my projects publicly about two months ago.

Not the polished version — the real version. The problem statement, the wrong turns, the client feedback that stung a little, the moment things clicked.

Here's what I've learned.

## It makes you think more clearly

When you know someone might read your reasoning, you reason better.

I've caught myself making decisions I couldn't actually justify — just going with a gut feeling that turned out to be laziness. Writing it down publicly forced me to either justify it or change it.

## It builds trust faster than a portfolio

A portfolio shows what you built. A build log shows how you think.

For clients and recruiters, the how is more valuable than the what. Anyone can list projects. Not everyone can show you the problem → decision → outcome chain clearly and honestly.

## The discomfort is the point

The posts where I explain what went wrong are the ones that get the most responses.

Nobody connects with "I built a thing and it worked." Everyone connects with "here's what I misunderstood, here's what it cost me, here's what I'd do differently."

That discomfort — the one that makes you want to edit out the failures — is exactly where the value is.

## What I'm still figuring out

How to write about client projects without exposing things they'd prefer private. How to be honest about uncertainty without sounding unconfident. How to do this consistently when a project is hard and I don't feel like writing.

Still working on it. But publicly.
